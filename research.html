Research

Welcome to my research page! I am passionate about exploring online social networks, focusing on the algorithms that mediate our connections to each other. My work spans multiple disciplines, leveraging machine learning, algorithmic audits, and network science to address key challenges in computational social science.

Research Interests

  • Social Networks: I'm interested in how we relate to one another, and the complex relationships that can be embedded into simple graphs.
  • Machine Learning: I'm interested in how machine learning can be a useful tool for answering research questions, and how it can be useful for people in particularly information-dense environments.
  • Multimodal Data Analysis: I think it is time to move beyond simply text-based analysis of complex social phenomenon online, and I'm interested in how we can leverage the new tools at our disposal to do so.
  • Algorithmic Auditing: I'm fascinated by the process of examining algorithms for bias, fairness, and transparency, especially in the context of social media and recommendation systems.

Current Projects

Agent-based models for Personalized Timelines

Collaborators: Dr. Keith Burghardt, Prof. Kristina Lerman

To understand the dynamics of recommender systems in social media platforms that underpin personalized timelines. The objective is to compare how they mediate users' experience online and exposure to their networks. We demonstrate that a greedy algorithm works to minimize such exposure bias. We make use of Repast to implement a large-scale agent-based model of a simple X/Twitter-like environment.
